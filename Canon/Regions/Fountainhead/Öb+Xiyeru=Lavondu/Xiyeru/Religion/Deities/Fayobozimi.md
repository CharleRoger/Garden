**Fayobozimi** is one of the [[ībarovā]], celestial creator deities in [[Xiyeru religion]], associated with the planet [[Pharon]].

# Mythology

Fayobozimi was one of the first three [[ībarovā]] to emerge from [[Fururūʼupwa]] in the [[Xiyeru creation story#The early universe|early universe]], during which time they created the first physical forms, light-avatars used by the other creator deities to interact with the material universe.

During the creation of the planet [[Winter]], Fayobozimi created caves and forests and went on to become the custodian of [[Ūžāraza#Types|pwaraza]], one of the [[Ūžāraza|fundamental substances of the universe]]. They are the patron deity of the ground, astronomy, geometry, construction and deconstruction (particularly of villages), and leadership.

Fayobozimi bore three children with [[Famenekwi]]: **[[Ādūn]]**, **[[Āzava]]**, and **[[Āñurō]]**

# Name

The name *[[Xiri dictionary#fayobozimi|fa-yobo-zimi]]* means "who rules the stars", in reference to their arrangement of the stars in the sky to aid the transmission of ancestral knowledge across generations of [[Xiyeru]] people.

In [[Möxali]], they are called *[[Möxali dictionary#yubzím|Yubzím]]*, one of the six [[Zívon|lunar months]] of the [[Öcídñox]] calendar.
