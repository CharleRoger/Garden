**Famenekwi** is one of the [[ībarovā]], celestial creator deities in [[Xiyeru religion]], associated with the planet [[Tünd]].

# Mythology

Famenekwi was one of the second three [[ībarovā]] to emerge from [[Fururūʼupwa]] in the [[Xiyeru creation story#The early universe|early universe]]. They are said to have given yams to humanity and taught them how to cultivate them. [[Xiyeru]] ritual use of yams is centred around worship of Famenekwi.

They are the patron deity of yams, harvest, accounting, and sound.

Famenekwi bore three children with [[Fayobozimi]]: **[[Ādūn]]**, **[[Āzava]]**, and **[[Āñurō]]**.
# Name

The name *[[Xiri dictionary#famenekwi|fa-mene-kwi]]* means "who cultivates yams".

In [[Möxali]], they are called *[[Möxali dictionary#minkö|Minkö]]*, one of the six [[Zívon|lunar months]] of the [[Öcídñox]] calendar.
