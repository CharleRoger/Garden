**Fadanaūmu** is one of the [[ībarovā]], celestial creator deities in [[Xiyeru religion]], associated with the planet [[Scoria]].

# Mythology

Fadanaūmu was one of the first three [[ībarovā]] to emerge from [[Fururūʼupwa]] in the [[Xiyeru creation story#The early universe|early universe]] and is said to have created the flow of time, rivers and ocean swells, and granted humanity the ability to procreate.

They are the patron deity of time, water, children, procreation, and the custodian of [[Ūžāraza#Types|ūmuraza]], one of the [[Ūžāraza|fundamental substances of the universe]].

Fadanaūmu bore three children with [[Fabepwes]]: **[[Āweʼa]]**, **[[Āvāń]]**, and **[[Āhēre]]**.

# Name

The name *[[Xiri dictionary#fadanaūmu|fa-dana-ūmu]]* means "who leads water", in reference to their guiding of the waters of [[Winter]] to form rivers and ocean swells.

In [[Möxali]], they are called *[[Möxali dictionary#danom|Danom]]*, one of the six [[Zívon|lunar months]] of the [[Öcídñox]] calendar.
