**Uvon** is the primary [[Möxali]] name for [[Bigmoon]] as an astronomical entity, and also refers to each of its 16 named phases. Other names for the moon are used in other contexts, such as *[[Möxali dictionary#inbu|inbu]]*, the personified name of the moon used colloquially and in the retelling of [[Lavondu]] lore.

# Etymology

*[[Möxali dictionary#uvon|Uvon]]* comes from the [[Xiri]] term *[[Xiri dictionary#uvāń|uvāń]]*, the plural form of *[[Xiri dictionary#fāń|fāń]]* used to describe [[Bigmoon]] and its phases.

# Lunar phases

[[Bigmoon|Bigmoon's]] 64-day synodic cycle is traditionally divided into 16 named phases by the [[Xiyeru]] and [[Lavondu]] peoples. The [[Möxali]] names for these are listed below:

1. *[[Möxali dictionary#xúvñu|Xúvñu]]* "darkness" (new moon)
2. *[[Möxali dictionary#yubñon|Yubñon]]* "reascension"
3. *[[Möxali dictionary#ulon|Ulon]]* "weaving"
4. *[[Möxali dictionary#úben|Úben]]* "lacquering"
5. *[[Möxali dictionary#sulno|Sulno]]* "sinking (canoe)"
6. *[[Möxali dictionary#nanan|Nanan]]* "feasting"
7. *[[Möxali dictionary#qirin|Qirin]]* "blooming"
8. *[[Möxali dictionary#qöyon|Qöyon]]* "persistence"
9. *[[Möxali dictionary#ölinklos|Ölinklos]]* "night-time sun" (full moon)
10. *[[Möxali dictionary#jiren|Jiren]]* "draining"
11. *[[Möxali dictionary#dúzun|Dúzun]]* "carving"
12. *[[Möxali dictionary#vacton|Vacton]]* "planting"
13. *[[Möxali dictionary#köxno|Köxno]]* "rising/floating (canoe)"
14. *[[Möxali dictionary#kíjin|Kíjin]]* "sharpening"
15. *[[Möxali dictionary#sumen|Sumen]]* "unravelling"
16. *[[Möxali dictionary#yuben|Yuben]]* "abdication"
