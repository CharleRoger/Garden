The **Tólóch** ([[Várdóch]]: [ˈtʰoːɮoːχ] "our people") are a culture and people group inhabiting much of western [[Chayadzó|Chayazó]] mainly in coastal areas and much of the surrounding hilly areas. They mainly speak [[Várdóch]], one of the largest members of the Tolian language family.

## Etymology

The word _Tólóch_ is derived from the word _tóle_ "people group, the people" and the first person plural possessive suffix _-óch_.

## History

Starting around 0 AGS, the Tólóch entered a new period of maritime trade mainly with the [[Ásadebasa]] and ultimately [[Cādereu|Cadereu]]. The Ásadebasa had come into contact with Cadereu centuries prior and by this point has already adopted key novel technologies from there, namely bronze production and writing. Copper smelting had already been long practiced on most of [[Dzòzò]] but due to the lack of tin deposits, bronze could not previously be produced. As such, continuous long distance trade became a necessity if bronze production was to be sustained. Such trade also lead to the introduction of various other cultural and material goods, most notably writing and of course also lead to certain new goods being exported to Cadereu.

The establishment of this new extended trade network also had a strong social impact on the Tólóch. The coastal areas ended up in a newfound position of leverage since they could largely control the availability of tin. This period also saw a significant increase in population size and the development of the first true citystates on the coast.
