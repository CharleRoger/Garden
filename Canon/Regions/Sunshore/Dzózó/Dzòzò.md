**Dzózó** ([[Ásademóku]]: [ˈdzoːzoː]) is a volcanically active archipelago located 370km off the coast of southern [[Cadereu]] and north of [[K'tlagn]]. It is part of the [[Sunshore]] region. Dzózó is about 1675km in length but fairly narrow being only 170km across at its widest. 62% of its land area is contained within the largest island, [[Chayadzó]].

## Etymology

The name in Ásademóku (_Dzózó_) is derived from reduplication of the word _dzó_ "island". The word can also be used a general term for a group of islands.

## Culture and Language

_Main articles: [[Ásadebasa]], [[Tólóch]], [[Ásademóku]], [[Várdóch]]_

Dzózó is home to a variety of cultures and cultural practices. These various groups naturally also speak many different languages and dialects.

## Geography

The islands making up Dzózó are generally mountainous and most are volcanically active. The tallest mountains are located on [[Chayazó]] where they reach heights of around 3,800 m.

### Seismic Activity and Volcanism

Since Dzózó is located on an active plate boundary it experiences frenquent earthquakes and occasionally tsunamis and volcanic eruptions. Tsunamis generally affect the eastern areas more heavily.

## Climate

Seasonal and diurnal temperature variation is generally small due to the close proximity of the ocean but is slightly larger in inland areas. The lowlands tend to see warm to hot temperatures throughout the year across the archipelago but temperatures are somewhat higher in the north compared to the south. Variation on the basis of altitude has a much greater impact in comparison. Very high altitude regions regularly experience snowfall especially during winter. The peaks of the highest mountains, mostly found on Chayadzó, may even be covered by snow and ice in the summer.

Precipitation varies significantly between seasons with most rainfall in the winter dry-season. The eastern side of most of the islands receives significantly more rainfall than the western side but the southern-most islands generally receive higher rainfall on their western side instead. This difference is most pronounced on Chayadzó due to the large mountain range running across the island. The east receives some of the highest precipitation out of any region within the islands whereas the west is mostly semi-arid to arid.
