There are several different Ran terms that may be translated as "King" or "Monarch", originating from different periods. 

# Tir

This was the original word for a king or a chief among the Ran. For example, [[Taspi]] and [[Feculky]] were each led by a king called a tir (plural tiraq, tiryq, or tireq). In Serquky, a common form of address for a king was icir. "Tirqela" referred to a viceroy and was used by the leader in [[Kypky]] between the [[Taspi-Kypky wars]] and later by preferred heirs.

The two [[Tot#Tot Cenukki|Totyq Cenukkiq]] eventually took over the responsibilities of the king in Taspi. After the [[Totky-Feculky wars]], both Kypky and Feculky were left without a king. The remaining tiraq around the Pilam were brought under the two Totyq Cenukkiq, the era of Totky hegemony began, giving birth to idea of Totkywa, the Ran cultural region conceived of as the land which paid Totky tributes and where Totky was supreme.
