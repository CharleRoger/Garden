For centuries, the chants were preserved as oral traditions among the nonatasyq, with a taboo against writing them down. As such, there are several versions of the chants, and the text given is just one reconstruction. Variations may be given in braces. The chants are listed using their number in the calendar and the traditional title, which often matches the incipit. The chants picked are famous examples of the five main categories found among the chants. These chants act as relatively clear representatives, unlike other chants whose categorization is disputed due to mixing various elements.

## King Melna (Chant 32)

King Melna, did you think
you would be like your father
King Melna, did you think
a good king rules alone?

Past the sea in Kypky
there was a man named Laxo
and though he did not know it
he would make a better King.

King Melna, did you think
you could arrest the Tot Cenukki
King Melna, did you think
that people would agree?
{or: *that the gods would agree?*}

Past the sea in Kypky
Laxo consulted his Tot Cenuk
and they were both outraged
so they left with an army

King Melna, did you think
you were now safe
Melna, did you not know
you were in mortal danger?

That side of the sea in Taspi
Laxo landed on the shore
his forces fought bravely
yet could not make it in

Melna, did you not know
your army would be useless?
Melna, did you not know
the threat was inside the walls?

That side of the sea in Taspi
people saw Laxo's bravery
Melna went into the Temple
but the One did not let him leave
{or: *but the gods did not let him leave}

Melna, did you not know
you would not be a father
Melna, did you not know
a bad king dies alone?

## To the whole world (Chant 51)

To the whole world, for everyone to hear
I proclaim the One by playing the flute
so break my flute in half
and I proclaim the One by playing the strings
so break my strings in half
and I proclaim the One by playing the bars
so break my bars in half
and I proclaim the One by singing my songs
so cut my throat in half
and I proclaim the One by dying my death

I proclaim the One by flute, 
strings, bars, song and death
but the last is the loudest
to the whole world, for everyone to hear.

## The wise prayer (Chant 75)

O (*exe*) Moferrumca Xal, who has always been xal, are xal, and will always be xal - guard me from blindless and help me see the truth. Now I take these fruit and divide (*xaloty*) them: profane fruit for the profane people, xal fruit for the xal gods. Like for like: profane for profane and xal for xal. And as I have given, I trust you give. O (*exe*) One, who has guarded me from blindless and helped me see the truth - I praise you!

## The night (Chant 256)


[this chant is typically sung during or after nightfall. afterwards, people stay in the nonatashouse for the night. all lights are turned off and no one may speak, eat, sleep, or sing. when the sun rises again, "The day" (Chant 1) is sung, lights are turned on, and people may socialize or leave and celebrate outside.]
