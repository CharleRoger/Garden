By Sertaxa, a largely secular philosopher from early second century AGS:
> And there are those who left the temples, meeting in normal houses. It is said they worship not "Fecul", but "One". They respond that they worship the one divinity [xalca] common to all gods and thus honor all gods. Much suspicion has been directed towards them, especially from the totyq, for they meet only among their own kind and sing secret [[nona|songs]] of praise no one else may hear. Yet they are invariably from among the families of the Wise, and in good standing with the King.

By Raxa, who installed as king after the first [[Totky-Feculky wars|Totky-Feculky war]]:
>Given in the tenth year of King Raxa of [[Feculky]] and approved by the totyq. Recite this in the town square and behind the doors of the nonatashouses: all nonatasyq must leave [same word as in "leave the city"] singing and praise Fecul in the temple or leave the city. After 12 days, any nonatas singing in the city of Fecul will be xal in the name of Fecul. For they [singular] have given Fecul nothing, and have brought great wrath upon Feculky, they will be given to Fecul. Should anyone stay or help a nonatas stay, they [singular] will be struck by all my forces.

The following two were given during the Feculky uprising:

> Approved by the totyq and given in the 25th year of King Raxa of Feculky. Having seen the recent happenings in Kypky and the will of the city, we appoint tot Tegen as the Tot Cenukki in Feculky, the first of the totyq. We order all soldiers and to swear an oath to Tegen, such that any soldier who disobeys him shall be xal and shall given to Fecul. Tegen's holy [xal] face shall be veiled and must not be seen by anyone but another tot. Should this rule be broken, we decree to punish the offender: by the perforation of the eyes if accidental, by death if intentional. For having seen by accident, the eyes are xal, but having seen on purpose, the mind too is xal. May the people be loyal to him and thank Fecul for his wisdom [pun; Tegen means wisdom].

> Given in the first year of Tegen, Tot Cenukki. Recite this to the soldiers, so that they may avoid the wrath of Fecul:
> If the nonatas plays the flute 
>    cut the flute in half
> If the nonatas plucks strings
>    cut the strings in half
> If the nonatas hits bars
>    cut the bars in half
> If the nonatas sings
>    cut the throat in half
> Do this and no more. Always act gently so as to encourage them in their ways, for they may yet leave them. But if they do not heed your warnings, act firmly and do not regret it.
