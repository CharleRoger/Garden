# Astmergian Empire

From Winterpedia

The **Astmergian Empire** is generally considered the first real empire of [Oksandanderi](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Oksandanderi "Oksandanderi"). It was the first state, which united the majority of the population of Oksandanderi under its rule. The empire was founded by [Astmerge the Great](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Astmerge_the_Great "Astmerge the Great") in [164 AGS](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/2nd_century "2nd century"). It went into decline after the death of Astmerge himself, after which it is often just called the **Astmergian Dominion**. Its definite end was with the conquest and destruction of [Kalotgane](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Kalotgane?action=edit&redlink=1 "Kalotgane (page does not exist)") in [267 AGS](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/3rd_century "3rd century"). The Astmergian Empire remains one of the most important events in the historical consciousness of the people of Oksandanderi. It is considered the foremost example of an empire by which most other empires are inspired by. Several other states have claimed succession to this empire, such as the [Neo-Astmergian Empire] or the [Makhatganda].

## Origins

### Dominion of Vërev

During the 1st and 2nd century the city of [[Vërev]] came to considerable power and established a dominion over parts of the Yarlish Lowlands. During the Second Dynasty of Vërev "Second Dynasty of Vërev (page does not exist)") they made a pivotal and long-lasting alliance with the city of Tarin. The phase of conquests lasting until the founding of the Astmergian Empire itself is generally regarded as the Vërevian Wars "Vërevian Wars (page does not exist)"), during which Vërev conquered the cities of Imnagal, Kamag and the Kingdom of Erme and Vukhun, as well as other territories. It culminated in the grand conquest by Astmerge himself.

### Conquests of Astmerge the Great

The declaration of the Empire was preluded by Astmerge's conquest of the Lowlands, which was completed with the subjugation of Kamag in 161 AGS. Astmerge had a great palace being built to celebrate his victory. The palace would later become the foundation for the city of Yardungane, which was fittingly named the monument to victory. After his victories in the Lowlands he turned his gaze towards the Highlands and towards the city of Pakkan itself. The city of Pakkan surrended to Astmerge, but the in the following weeks he suffered a defeat against the Kingdom of Kamal, which forced him to remain in Pakkan. After this he set out to conquer the Highlands themselves. After finally returning from campaign he had the city of Kalotgane built and declared himself **Lord of the Lowlands and the Highlands**.

## The Reign of Astmerge

The height of the Astmergian Empire lasted from the 160s till the death of Astmerge in 210. This time is generally considered the _Astmergian Empire-proper_. After 164 AGS, there was only a single unsuccessful attempt at further territorial expansion. In 188 AGS Astmerge was defeated by an alliance of kingdoms in the Kuperi "Kuperi (page does not exist)") region. During most of the almost four decades of Astmergian power, most energy was spent on consolidating rule over such a vast realm. New cities were built in several places to manage the newly established bureaucracy. The capital Kalotgane itself was one such foundation. The other cities are: Yardungane, Khisandan, Altëngane, Igemarv, Kamalgane, Vánarv and the lost city of Tartarv.

The size and infrastructure of the empire fascilitated trade and brought newfound prosperity to its inhabitants. The new cities grew rapidly and became centres of trade and education. In general the time from 190 to 210 AGS is considered a Golden Age. This time of peace and prosperity contrasts with the decades of strife, which would follow the death of Astmerge.

## Structure

It is said, that the Astmergian Empire consisted of eight provinces, the so called **Kathandu Kandam**. What these eight provinces were exactly remains unclear however. Most assume that they were based on the eight Astmergian cities, from where they were governed. Astmerge established a new form of bureaucracy for his empire. The governors of each province were chosen by himself and would only answer to him. The same went for military officers. The older nobility could retain their privileges and titles, but was not entitled to a place at his court. The position of governors was also not hereditary. This excluded a large part of the old elite from power, which was by design. Astmerge wanted not to have vassals, but to create an empire with a structure of its own. Thus he made himself and his government independent from the old nobility. While this was an ingenious design, it also created the later downfall of the empire.

## Decline

### Astmergian Dominion

Astmerge died in 210 AGS. He chose [Kesërn](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Kes%C3%ABrn?action=edit&redlink=1 "Kesërn (page does not exist)") to become his successor. Kesërn was married to his eldest daughter [Khayanmari](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Khayanmari?action=edit&redlink=1 "Khayanmari (page does not exist)"). By doing this he passed over his eldest son [Yardunmud](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Yardunmud?action=edit&redlink=1 "Yardunmud (page does not exist)"). Kesërn was chosen for his abilities and friendship and familial bonds to Astmerge, who allegedly had little faith in Yardunmud. This choice was contested almost immediately. In Vërev Yardunmud was made king of Vërev roughly at the same time. The Vërevian nobility claimed, that the Astmergian Empire was in fact still the Vërevian Empire and that the old laws were still in place. At first the tensions between Vërev and Kalotgane did not outright erupt, except that Vërev and Tarin declined any form of taxes to Kalotgane. Vërev began to form alliances with other cities in the Lowlands, and together they largely blocked any sort of allegiance to Kalotgane. The withheld revenues caused many of the new cities to decline in wealth. The general infrastructure of the empire began to break down eventually. However in many places the local bureaucracy survived and governed their respective provinces like separated kingdoms. This almost decentralised network of Astmergian cities was called the Astmergian Dominion.

### Astmergian Civil War

As long as [Kesërn](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Kes%C3%ABrn?action=edit&redlink=1 "Kesërn (page does not exist)") was still alive attempts at outright conquest of Kalotgane failed. One such invasion was attempted in 222 AGS, another in 228. However each campaign caused deep injuries to the Astmergian Dominion. Kesërn passed away in 233 AGS. This caused an event called the **Desertation of Kalotgane**, where almost half the population of Kalotgane left the city in a single year. Kalotgane was left completely defenseless after that, but not yet conquered. Inside the city ruled Khayanmari as empress-regent together with her sons.

Vërev used this opportunity to conquer the other Astmergian cities. In 235, Kamalgane was devastated and almost completely abandoned. Further to the northwest arose the [Kingdom of Erme and Vukhun](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Erme_and_Vukhun "Kingdom of Erme and Vukhun") anew to old strength. It subjugated the city of Igemarv and deported its populace. Other places fell to a similar state. Only the midlands survived united during this time, but there too local rebellions weakened the overall state. The city of Pakkan declared its independence in 244, thus separating Vánarv and Kalotgane additionally. The city in the mountains remained the only strong ally of Kalotgane for a while. The conquest of the other cities also lead to more people migrating upwards the [Yarla river](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Yarla_River?action=edit&redlink=1 "Yarla River (page does not exist)") towards Vánarv. The 250s saw a renewal of unrest and war. Several princes of Kalotgane fell in battle during this time and in 252 was [Kalyardun II](https://winter.miraheze.org/wiki/Kalyardun_II?action=edit&redlink=1 "Kalyardun II (page does not exist)"). of Kalotgane also killed, leading to his young son Yardunadi becoming ruler over Kalotgane. For a few years it seemed that Kalotgane could actually resurface, as they successfully regained control over Imnagal and formed a pact with Pakkan and Vánarv, which united the Midlands for a short while.

### Fall of Kalotgane

Despite some successes, the Astmergian Dominion was ultimately doomed. Kalotgane could not reestablish its control over the Lowlands, while Vërev and Tarin had established control over several key cities. Kalotgane was ruled by the aging empress Khayanmari, who held regency over her grandson Yardunadi. In 258 AGS Khayanmari passed away. Her death would additionally seal the fate of Kalotgane. In 267 the cities of Vërev and Tarin amassed a great force and set out to conquer and destroy Kalotgane. The city was sacked and almost completely depopulated and would fall into obscurity in the coming years. The last emperor was taken prisoner and held as hostage in Vërev until his death in 314. He would die unmarried and childless.
