## Brief introduction to Kóty

## From distant past to Forth and towards Third century BGS

First sight of humans in Kóty is hypothesized to be at the 40th century BGS with early Koyanic people starting to leave their homeland around the Second Hundredth century BGS spreading and diverging into several phyla being those Arklobu-Qachkav and Qak towards Eastern Kóty and Kátic towards Western Kóty and the Khey Desert. Not only this Upic peoples stablished themselves in Western Kóty around similar centuries.

Second clues of new ethno-linguistic groups in Kóty happen in the 14th century BGS when Hautaric and Dreic people arise at the same time, and move towards Kóty, only a portion of Hautaric and Dreic people leaving towards the desert at the 3rd to 2nd century BGS thus stablishing communities, like the Rotak and Rabak of hautaric peoples and Khey people of Dreic origin.

Atteric people is finally the last ethno-linguistic ground in Kóty to arise and migrate, they chose a different path, going towards the islands in the continent of Kóty, several phyla of different cultures stablished there until they migrated back to land, those who stayed are the Ansang and Yuge people, those who went to the continent are the Ekerre and Vakeye people.

## Third to First century BGS

From 3rd to 1st century BGS Vakeye people went towards the khey desert, new languages branch off Dreic, Shote and Berkke, several other languages branch off in Upic, Koyanic and Hautaric.
