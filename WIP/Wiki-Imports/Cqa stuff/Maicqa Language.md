## Phonotactics

Yet to be elaborated on much; the rough gist is

(C₁)V(C₂), where

- C₁ is any consonant
- V is any vowel
- C₂ is any non-click consonant

with the timing note that C₁VC₂+C₂VC₃ does not take on any special timing despite two adjacent C₂s.

## Morphology

Verbal phrases always open with articles indicating their head verb's inherent class; these Verb Classifiers (VCLs) indicate positivity/negativity and possible inversion. VCLs also reçeive the markers for moods and some aspects, and can also indicate valency and further aspectual information by marker, or on their own through tendencies/restrictions of the class they indicate.

In some instances, derivations may be made by using VCLs to force an existing verb into a new class. _For example, verbs of attendance (ATT) describing unusual experiences regularly get derivatives in (/get moved to) other classes to indicate desired or factual normality._

VCLs initially arose as auxiliaries disambiguating between homophonous verbs, but have since shifted in role and become mandatory for all verbs. They are not classified as auxiliaries, because they are strictly bound by _arbitrary_ classes inherent to any verbs. When considering historical developments, however, the question of whether class is inherent to verbs or if classes are sets of behaviours prescribed by VCLs, is often ambiguous.

For a full overview of the verb classes, see [[Maicqa Verb Classes]].
