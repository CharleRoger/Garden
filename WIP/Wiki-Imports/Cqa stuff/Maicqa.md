The **Maicqa** ([[mül]]: /mɑj.ŋ͡ǂɑ/, lit. _"common island"_, _[people of] shared islands_) are a diaspora of people. They inhabit [[Cqa]] and speak a language of the same name, the [[Maicqa Language]].

## People-naming conventions

Generally, only you or a representative (lawyer, whānau...) use your full name about you; random strangers don't know what's going on with where you're originally from, and it's none of their business. The full form of a person's name as far as most are concerned, is their current home, given name, and a title/honorific. Titles are typically based on fields of expertise, i.e. profession, but may also be taken from one's relation to the person (such as "my apprentice" instead of "gull farmer"). Address in day-to-day conversation usually uses given names andor titles, depending on the contextual need for disambiguation. Full names are structured as follows:

- _tqa=_ island of origin, with excessive case clitic
- _da=_ current home, with adverbial clitic
- given- or nickname
- title, profession, or other honorific

_Example person: tqalai dotan xqom söc /ǃɑ.dɑj dɔ.tɑn ǁɔŋ tøc/_

So, how about those job-based titles? Let's have a look at a few common monosyllabic ones. A full list may be found [[here]].

- söc /tøc/ — ≈"patcher", works primarily with heavy canvas repair and maintenance (probably also proficient in tailoring and snot plate maintenance).
- cgaz /ǃ¡ɑɟ/ — ≈"caretaker", specialized in maintaining mollusks and harvesting snot (often also transporting it). also good with glue.
- mad /mɑɹ/ — ≈"wanderer", a sort of herald, delivery service, merchant, and mollusk herder for hire all in one.
- nüj /nʉj/ — ≈"(fiber) farmer", someone who tends to plants/algae/etc and smaller mollusks, specializing in manufacturing excess canvas/snot on the side.
- tqez /ǃɛɟ/ — ≈"(food) farmer, chef", someone who primarily works with various ways of making food and has making an excess of it as their main role.
- zir /ɟir/ — ≈"courier", local transport person, lugger of things, sort of general helper with manual/mollusk labour (often spends a lot of time moving food and water).

## Place-naming conventions

The Maicqa typically name their places with semi-flexible descriptive phrases, most of which are based on land use, or after prominent people who live there. A place dubbed "nobody's" is an option, even if somebody lives there. Broader areas are named from roughly the same register of names as people, but receive place-type classifiers instead of titles. Locations within these areas are typically descriptive, relying on ownership and location. Within Xgom söc's Xgom nalak (small vertical island cluster), the red island with a distillery would likely simply be described as _the red island with a distillery_. Registries might ignore this and refer to the area as their own arbitrary personal name and description. This may result in recorded place names hinting at different social dynamics than local place names. As settlements grow, it's not unheard of for a single "neighbour's exonym" to take broader areas, with alternate names potentially becoming local in-culture relics or shibboleth rather than actual commonly used terms.

Thanks to some evidential/emphatic particles, another fun phenomenon is when an endonym about something hidden from outsiders ends up leaking out and becoming the later widespread name. Think "xgom's distillery (but don't tell anyone)", but then everyone knows it as that because it's in a registry now.
